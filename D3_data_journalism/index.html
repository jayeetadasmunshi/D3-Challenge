Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

There appears to be a positive correlation between poverty and lack of healthcare. Massachusetts has the lowest percentage of population lacking healthcare while Texas has the dubious distinction of 25% of population lacking healthcare. Southern states rank low in health care and high in poverty, while the northern states are in the opposite spectrum.

There appears to be a negative correlation between median household income and lack of healthcare. Median income is highest for Maryland, followed by Washington DC and Massachusetts. These states have lowest percentage of population lacking healthcare. Texas remains an outlier, with median income similar to Vermont, but 4 times more people lacking healthcare.

Median age of the population lies between 34 and 44 years. Older population in Vermont enjoys best healthcare, while Florida has more than twice the number lacking healthcare. Younger population in the southern states tend to lack healthcare coverage with Texas leading the pack with quarter of 35 year olds lacking healthcare.
